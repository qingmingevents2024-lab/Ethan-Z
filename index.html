Violins

A violin is a string instrument that has 4 strings.

It has quite a few parts

A lot of them are: Scroll: .Pegs: Turning these adjusts the tension of the strings to tune the violin. Nut: The small ridge that holds the strings at the correct spacing as they run down to the fingerboard. Fingerboard: The smooth black board where the player presses their fingers to change notes. Body: The resonant wooden chamber (belly, back, and sides/ribs) that amplifies the sound. F-holes: The cutouts on the front of the body that allow sound to project outward. Bridge: A curved piece of maple that holds the strings up and transfers their vibrations into the body. Strings: Usually tuned to G, D, A, and E (from low to high).Tailpiece: Anchors the strings to the bottom of the violin. Fine Tuners: Small metal screws attached to the tailpiece for precise tuning adjustments. Chin Rest: A rest attached to the side of the instrument for comfortable jaw placement. End button: The small wooden knob at the base of the violin that anchors the tailpiece.
